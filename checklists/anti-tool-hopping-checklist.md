## Decision framework

Before switching tools, answer:

1. **Is the current tool broken?** (Not "could be better" — actually broken)
   - Yes → Fix it or switch
   - No → Don't switch

2. **Will switching save time?** (Include learning time)
   - Yes → Consider switching
   - No → Don't switch

3. **Have you used the new tool before?**
   - Yes → Maybe switch
   - No → Don't switch

---

## Red flags

If you're thinking any of these, STOP:

- "This other framework is better"
- "Everyone uses X, I should too"
- "Let me just try X for a minute"
- "X has more features"
- "X is more popular"

**Reality check:** Your users don't care what tool you use. They care if your product works.

---

## The 3-5 tool rule

Your project uses 2-4 tools maximum. Each must have a clear job:

1. [Frontend tool]: [purpose]
2. [Backend/AI tool]: [purpose]
3. [Deploy tool]: [purpose]
4. [Tool 4]: [purpose] (optional)
5. [Tool 5]: [purpose] (optional)

No optional tools. No "nice to have" tools.

---

## What to do instead

Instead of switching tools:
- Fix the problem with your current tool
- Ask for help with your current tool
- Work around the limitation
- Ship what you have

---

## Emergency switch

Only switch if:
- Current tool is actually broken (not just hard)
- You've spent 2+ hours trying to fix it
- The new tool is one you already know
- Switching will save 1+ day of work
